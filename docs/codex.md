# Kurukshetra Codex

A working reference for the game's roster and weapons, drawn from the Mahabharata first, then wider canonical Hindu myth (Ramayana, the Puranas, Devi Mahatmya, the Vedic layer) where it can slot in as neutrals or cross-epic guests.

This is a design bible, not a scripture. Where the texts disagree (the Critical Edition versus regional recensions, or later retellings), I have gone with the widely attested version and flagged the shaky bits. Nothing here is a final card yet. The point is to see the whole field, tag each entry with a **card hook**, and then choose what to build.

## How to read this

**Tier** uses the epic's own warrior ranking, from the pre-war assessments in the Udyoga and Bhishma Parvas:

- **R** Rathi: a capable single-chariot warrior.
- **AR** Atirathi: worth many rathis, a front-line champion.
- **MR** Maharathi: worth many atirathis, an army-breaker.
- **AMR** Atimaharathi: the very top, used loosely for the handful who tower over everyone (and for divine beings). This tier is more a fan and retelling convention than a strict Critical-Edition term, so treat it as flavour.

Ratings were political. Bhishma, Drona, and Krishna each rated the warriors differently, and Bhishma's famous slight of Karna as an *ardharatha* (half a chariot) was an insult, not a neutral measure. I have avoided the "can fight N maharathis at once" multipliers from popular retellings, since those are not reliably in the text.

**Side** is the deck a card would sit in: Pandava, Kaurava, Deva, Asura, Rishi, or Neutral.

## Part V. Devas, avatars, and cosmic figures (canonical, cross-text)

The natural "Deva" faction, and the divine fathers link them straight to the Pandavas.

**The Trimurti**
- **Brahma** the creator, source of the Brahmastra line.
- **Vishnu** the preserver, wielder of the Sudarshana Chakra, source of the Vaishnavastra and Narayanastra.
- **Shiva** the destroyer (Rudra, Pashupati), source of the Pashupatastra and the Trishula.

**The Dashavatara of Vishnu** (a whole sub-set on their own)
- Matsya (fish), Kurma (turtle), Varaha (boar), **Narasimha** (man-lion, slays Hiranyakashipu), **Vamana** (dwarf, humbles Bali), **Parashurama**, **Rama**, **Krishna** (some lists put Balarama or the Buddha here), **Kalki** (yet to come).

**The Lokapalas and elemental gods** (the divine fathers among them)
- **Indra** king of the devas, the Vajra, father of Arjuna.
- **Yama** lord of death and dharma, father of Yudhishthira.
- **Vayu** wind, father of Bhima (and of Hanuman).
- **The Ashvins** twin physicians, fathers of Nakula and Sahadeva.
- **Surya** the sun, father of Karna, giver of the Akshaya Patra.
- **Agni** fire, gave Arjuna the Gandiva at the burning of Khandava.
- **Varuna** the waters, source of the Varunastra.
- **Kubera** lord of wealth and the yakshas.
- **Chandra** the moon, **Ishana**, **Nirriti** round out the directional guardians.

**Goddesses and other powers**
- **Durga / Kali** the war-goddess (Arjuna invokes her before the battle in some recensions).
- **Ganga** Bhishma's mother, **Lakshmi**, **Saraswati**, **Parvati**.
- **Hanuman** the deathless, on Arjuna's banner (Kapidhwaja) and a bridge to the Ramayana.
- **Garuda** Vishnu's mount and the natural counter to serpent weapons.

## Part VII. Divyastras, the divine weapons (the core of the ask)

An astra is a weapon invoked by mantra and presided over by a deity. This is the near-complete field from the Mahabharata plus the well-known cross-epic ones. Effects and counters are as the texts describe them, kept in game-usable terms.

| Astra | Presiding power | Known wielders | Effect | Counter / note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Brahmastra** | Brahma | Arjuna, Karna, Ashwatthama, Drona, Rama, Indrajit | Unerring mass destruction of a target or region, with famine and drought where it lands. | Answered only by another Brahmastra. Two loosed at once threaten the world, so they must be withdrawn. |
| **Brahmashirsha** | Brahma | Arjuna, Ashwatthama | Four times a Brahmastra, a wider annihilation. | Ashwatthama could not recall his, so he turned it on Uttaraa's womb, Krishna revived the child (Parikshit). |
| **Brahmanda astra** | Brahma | rarely, the most extreme | Said to hold the power to end a cosmic cycle. | Effectively the ceiling of the Brahma line. |
| **Pashupatastra** | Shiva (Rudra) | Arjuna (won after the Kirata duel) | Can destroy creation itself. Never to be used on a lesser foe. | The "I win, but it burns out" card. Forbidden against the weak. |
| **Narayanastra** | Vishnu (Narayana) | Ashwatthama (once) | Rains countless weapons, and grows stronger the more you resist. | The only counter is total submission: drop your weapons and do not fight. Usable once. |
| **Vaishnavastra** | Vishnu | Bhagadatta, Indrajit | Infallible, seeks the named life. | Krishna took Bhagadatta's onto his chest, where it became a garland. Even this bows to a greater divine presence. |
| **Sudarshana Chakra** | Vishnu / Krishna | Krishna | A personal discus that removes any one target, ignoring protections. | Not a mantra-astra, it is Krishna's own weapon. |
| **Vajra** | Indra | Indra | The thunderbolt, slew Vritra. Raw annihilating strike. | Forged from the sage Dadhichi's bones. |
| **Vasavi Shakti (Shakti / Amogha)** | Indra | Karna | A one-time unerring kill dart, given in trade for Karna's armour. | Karna was forced to spend it on Ghatotkacha, saving Arjuna. Single use. |
| **Aindrastra** | Indra | Arjuna | A shower of arrows or rain, a mass area attack. | Elemental line. |
| **Agneyastra** | Agni | many | Unquenchable fire, blinding heat. | Countered by the Varunastra. |
| **Varunastra** | Varuna | many | Torrents of water, floods, storms. | Counters and quenches the Agneyastra. |
| **Vayavyastra** | Vayu | Arjuna | Gales that scatter armies and disperse clouds. | Counters weapons that rely on smoke or cloud. |
| **Nagastra / Nagapasha** | the Nagas | Karna (Ashwasena), Indrajit | A serpent-arrow that seeks the head, or a noose of snakes that binds. | Countered by the Garudastra. Krishna sank the chariot so Karna's shot took the crown, not the head. |
| **Garudastra** | Garuda | Arjuna and others | Summons the eagle-force that devours serpents. | The hard counter to the Naga weapons. |
| **Suryastra / Adityastra** | Surya | Karna and others | Blinding light that dispels darkness and illusions. | Answers darkness and stealth astras. |
| **Yamyastra (Kaal)** | Yama | few | Invokes death itself over an area. | Among the deadliest deva-astras. |
| **Kauberastra / Antardhana** | Kubera | Arjuna | Makes the target or the self vanish, wealth and concealment. | Utility and stealth. |
| **Twashtar astra** | Tvashtri | Arjuna | Sows confusion so the enemy host turns on itself. | Mass debuff, friendly fire. |
| **Sammohana / Pramohanastra** | (delusion) | Arjuna | Puts an entire army to sleep or stupor (used at Virata). | Non-lethal mass disable. |
| **Parvatastra** | (mountains) | few | Drops a mountain on the foe. | Blunt area kill. |
| **Bhargavastra** | Parashurama (Bhargava) | Karna | A relentless storm of arrows, learned from Parashurama, immense against masses. | Karna's signature. |
| **Manavastra** | Manu | Rama and others | A strong directed strike, part of the sage-taught set. | Classic tutor astra. |
| **Mohanastra** | (enchantment) | Rama, Arjuna | Bewilders and disarms the enemy. | Pairs with Sammohana. |
| **Aishika (blade of grass)** | Brahma-empowered | Ashwatthama | A humble reed charged into a lethal astra during the night raid. | Shows any object can carry an astra. |
| **Twvarious deva bows' named shots** | see Part VIII | | Many "astras" in the text are simply the presiding god's named arrow. | Grouping note. |

Cross-epic astras worth noting from the Ramayana: Rama wields the **Brahmastra** (to slay Ravana), and Indrajit the **Brahmastra**, **Vaishnavastra**, and **Nagapasha**. These overlap the list above, which is the point: the divine armory is shared canon.

A caution for accuracy: the texts name many astras, and minor ones vary between recensions and retellings (spelling, presiding deity, exact effect). The rows above are the well-attested core. If we want a citation-grade version, that is a separate verification pass.

## Part VIII. Shastras and divine artifacts (the equipment)

Not invoked by mantra, these are the great named weapons and objects. Perfect as "boon" or "attach to a warrior" cards, which is also the direction we discussed for reworking astras: a weapon wielded by the warrior who owns it.

**Divine bows**
- **Gandiva**, Arjuna's, originally Brahma's, given by Agni at Khandava. Never fails its master.
- **Sharanga (Saranga)**, Vishnu's bow, carried by Krishna.
- **Pinaka (Ajagava)**, Shiva's bow.
- **Vijaya**, Indra's bow, given by Parashurama to Karna.
- **Kodanda**, Rama's bow.

**Maces (gada)**
- **Kaumodaki**, Vishnu and Krishna's mace.
- **Bhima's gada**, the great club of the strongest Pandava.
- **Duryodhana's gada**, his equal at the mace, the weapon of the final duel.

**Discus, trident, and blades**
- **Sudarshana Chakra**, Vishnu and Krishna's discus (also in Part VII).
- **Trishula**, Shiva's trident. **Pinaka's** counterpart in melee.
- **Nandaka**, Vishnu's sword.
- **Chandrahasa**, the moon-blade Shiva gave Ravana.

**Conches (shankha), the five Pandava voices plus Krishna's**
- **Panchajanya**, Krishna.
- **Devadatta**, Arjuna.
- **Paundra**, Bhima.
- **Anantavijaya**, Yudhishthira.
- **Sughosha**, Nakula.
- **Manipushpaka**, Sahadeva.

**Armor and vessels**
- **Kavacha and Kundala**, Karna's inborn golden armour and earrings from Surya, the source of his near-invulnerability until he gives them to Indra.
- **Akshaya Patra**, the inexhaustible vessel Surya gave the Pandavas.
- **Kapidhwaja**, Arjuna's banner bearing Hanuman.
- **Nandighosha**, Arjuna's chariot, driven by Krishna.

## Part IX. Card-design hooks (curses, boons, and vows into mechanics)

The richest cards are the conditional ones. A shortlist of the strongest hooks:

| Figure | Canon hook | Mechanic sketch |
|---|---|---|
| Bhishma | Icchamrityu | Cannot be removed until Shikhandi is on the board. (Built.) |
| Karna | Two curses plus Kavacha-Kundala | Armour blocks the first removal, mantra forgotten in the final round, wheel sinks in the decider. (Built.) |
| Drona | Lays down arms at false news | Immune until the "Ashwatthama is dead" card is played. (Built.) |
| Duryodhana | Diamond body but the thighs | Immune to removal except Bhima's vow. (Built.) |
| Abhimanyu | Enters but cannot exit the vyuha | Doomed if a formation card (Jayadratha) is present. (Built.) |
| Hiranyakashipu | Boon with a loophole | Immune except to a specific "neither man nor beast, neither day nor night" answer card. |
| Jarasandha | Rejoins when split | Revives unless killed by a "split" effect. |
| Shishupala | Hundred forgiven insults | Survives the first N removals, then dies to the next. |
| Raktabija | Blood clones | Spawns a copy whenever damaged, unless a "drink the blood" answer is out. |
| Ashwatthama | Deathless, cursed | Cannot be destroyed, only neutralized, wanders the board. |
| Narayanastra | Resistance feeds it | Damage scales with the enemy's board power, countered only by a "lay down arms" card. |
| Bhishma / Vasus | A Vasu incarnate | Bond with the eight Vasus for a Deva synergy. |

## Sources and honesty note

Built from widely attested Mahabharata canon (the Vyasa epic and its major parvas) and standard Puranic, Ramayana, Devi Mahatmya, and Vedic material. Where retellings diverge, I chose the common version and said so. Specific astra effects, presiding deities, and minor characters can shift between the Critical Edition and regional or modern retellings. Nothing here is a numeric balance decision, those come when we turn entries into cards. If we want a citation-grade version of any section (especially the divyastra table), that is a focused verification pass we can run.

## Implemented (as of the roster build)

The fighting roster is now in the game: 31 Pandava-side cards, 30 Kaurava-side, and 15 astras/tricks (76 total). Every combatant above who took the field is a card, including the half-asuras and rakshasas (Ghatotkacha, Anjanaparvan, Iravan, Alambusha, Alayudha), the Upapandavas, the wheel-guards, the Bahlika elders, the Avanti twins, and the allied kings. Traits use these engine keywords: `icchamrityu`, `immuneUntilPlayed`, `armor`, `diamond-body`, `deathless` (chiranjivi: Ashwatthama, Kripa, Yuyutsu), `nightGrowth` (rakshasa night-strength: Ghatotkacha, Alambusha, Alayudha, Kritavarma), and `bond` (squad synergy: twins, Upapandavas, Avanti, wheel-guards, kaurava-brothers, sun-line, rakshasa). Astras are invoked only by a warrior who knows them, with the counter-web (Naga/Garuda, Agni/Varuna, Brahma/Brahma, Brahmashirsha).

Balanced via the sim to Pandava ~50 / Kaurava ~48; the human plays second for a fair edge.
